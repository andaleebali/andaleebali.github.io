Segmenting and Classifying Roof Materials
in Bulawayo, Zimbabwe

Role: Student, University of Edinburgh

Project Summary

For my masters dissertation I looked at identifying and classifying roof materials across in Bulawayo, Zimbabwe using high-resolution satellite imagery (WorldView-2) and supervised classification techniques. The primary goal was to see if roof materials could be used as a proxy for understanding wealth distribution across the city.

Using machine learning methods (Random Forest and Decision Trees) in ArcGIS Pro and Google Earth Engine, I processed and analyzed imagery layers including RGB, near-infrared, and NDVI. Training data was collected through digitization and visual interpretation, with accuracy assessed using confusion matrices.

The final output was an interactive map and attribute layer showing predominant roof materials (e.g., metal, tile, concrete), which was integrated into the councilâ€™s internal dashboard systems.
