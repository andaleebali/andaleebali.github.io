About Me

I’m a Geospatial Analyst with a background spanning earth observation, spatial data science, and systems engineering. I hold a Master’s in Earth Observation and Geoinformation Management from the University of Edinburgh and a First-Class Honours degree in Mathematics. My professional experience includes working in local government (Gisborne District Council, NZ) and defence (BAE Systems, UK), where I developed spatial tools, automated workflows, and supported decision-making through data-driven insights.

My strengths lie in spatial problem-solving, Python scripting, and the design of intuitive GIS solutions using tools like ArcGIS Pro, ArcGIS Online, Field Maps, and Dashboards. I also bring a keen interest in environmental monitoring, land use, and the application of remote sensing for climate-related challenges. I enjoy translating complex data into accessible, actionable outputs and working across teams to solve real-world problems.

I’m passionate about using geospatial technology to support sustainable planning, biodiversity, and resilient infrastructure—and I’m always looking to learn and apply new tools to make a meaningful impact.
